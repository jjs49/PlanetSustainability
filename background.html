What do we want to accomplish?

Learning Objective

After completion of our interactive experience, the player should be able to make more conscious decisions regarding the environment. We want to highlight the small choices that the player can make in their everyday life to make a positive impact on the environment. We wanted our project to focus on education as its deliverable. We wanted to use the skills we had obtained to convey information to the user through an interactive medium.

Importance of Biodiversity

Biodiversity surrounds us in our everyday lives, although many of the actions that we make may not have a direct impact, many of the choices that we make hav an indirect influence. There are many areas in which our actions can directly influence biodiversity, below are a number of elements in which we focused on throughout the development of the environments in our project.

Gardening

Allowing and promoting wildflower growth in Ireland can contribute to the global benefits of the environment. Native horticulture can provide vital habitats for pollinating insects. Especially native wildflowers, as they provide shelter, food, homes and nectar for these insects as well as a larger variety of wildlife.

Supporting local animals

Due to urbanisation, there are more limited areas in which local animals can find sources of food. Installing food sources such as bird feeders can be an effective method of promoting wild birds in your area.

Shop consciously

Sourcing products from sustainable sources had prevent habitat degradation and fragmentation. Choose products with a sustainability certificate.

Travel methods

Ideally, we would all limit the amount of travel that we do as much as we can, both in proximity and frequency. If we do have to travel, we should all be conscious of the mode of transport that we take. For example, when organising a trip, try to restrict yourself to travel without flying. If we have to travel for work or education, consider a more environmentally responsible method. For example, instead of driving consider public transport, cycling or walking.

Food

The key to sustainable foods lie in the goods that we purchase. Shop local and shop seasonal. Lots of fruits and vegatables which are not local or in season result in large carbon footprints being produced because of their transportation. It is best to check the country of origin when selecting items that you are unsure of in the supoermarket.

Don't be passive

You can contact your local council, workplace or educational institute to find out what they are doing in terms of biodiversity. You can also organise something yourself if you can.

Don't stray from the path

While you're exploring and enjoying nature it is really important that you do not stray from designated paths. This is because your wandering may have a negative effect on the ecosystem that you are in and may damage the biodiversity of the area. To mitigate this risk, you should stay on the trails marked or follow a guide to see the beauty the area has to offer.

Don't believe everything that you are told

There is a lot of generalities as well as liberties that have been known to be taken in marketing. Be careful not to fall for misleading information about a company or products sustainability. Greenwashing can be an unsubstantiated claim which can mislead consumers into thinking that a company's products are environmentally friendly. To avoid this, you should always look out for the sustainability certificates as mentioned, or do some extra research into a bracnds sustainability on their website.

Watch what you are eating

It has been proven that the production of animal products has a big impact on biodiversity. Therefore you can consider limiting the amount of meat that you can consume each week.

Eat before you go shopping

When you shop when you're hungry, you can tend to purchase too much food as well as buying convenience foods. These foods are highly processed and have been known to affect biodiversity negatively. Try eating something small before you go to the supermarket.
